An aerodynamic stall occurs when the wing exceeds its critical angle of attack.

When the angle of attack becomes too high, airflow separates from the upper surface of the wing, causing a significant reduction in lift.

A stall can occur at any airspeed if the critical angle of attack is exceeded. Common causes include excessive pitch-up maneuvers, low-speed flight, and improper aircraft handling.

Pilots recover from stalls by reducing the angle of attack and increasing airflow over the wings. Stall training is an important part of pilot education.
