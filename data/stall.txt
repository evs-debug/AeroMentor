Definition:
An aerodynamic stall occurs when a wing exceeds its critical angle of attack.

How It Works:
When the angle of attack becomes too high, airflow separates from the upper surface of the wing, reducing lift significantly.

Common Causes:
- Excessive pitch-up maneuvers.
- Low-speed flight.
- Improper aircraft handling.
- Abrupt control inputs.

Recovery:
Pilots recover from stalls by reducing the angle of attack and increasing airflow over the wings.

Importance:
Stall recognition and recovery are essential pilot skills and are a major part of flight training.
