Definition:
The Boeing 747 is a wide-body airliner known for its distinctive upper deck and large passenger capacity.

Key Facts:
- Nicknamed the "Queen of the Skies."
- First entered service in 1970.
- Was the world's largest passenger aircraft for decades.
- Available in passenger and cargo variants.

Importance:
The Boeing 747 revolutionized long-distance air travel by making international flights accessible to more passengers.
