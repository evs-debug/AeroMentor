Definition:
The Boeing 747 is a wide-body four-engine airliner known for its distinctive upper-deck hump.

Specifications:
The aircraft was one of the first jumbo jets and revolutionized long-distance air travel.

Passenger Capacity:
Many configurations carry between 400 and 600 passengers.

Range:
Modern variants are capable of long-haul and intercontinental operations.

Engines:
The aircraft uses four high-bypass turbofan engines.

Importance:
The Boeing 747 transformed global aviation and became one of the most iconic aircraft in history.
