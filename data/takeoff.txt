Definition:
Takeoff is the phase of flight in which an aircraft accelerates along a runway and becomes airborne.

Key Facts:
- Requires sufficient thrust and lift.
- Performance depends on aircraft weight and weather.
- Pilots monitor speeds such as V1, VR, and V2.
- Requires careful runway and obstacle analysis.

Importance:
Takeoff is one of the most critical phases of flight and demands precise pilot performance.
