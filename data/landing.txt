Definition:
Landing is the phase of flight in which an aircraft returns to the ground and decelerates safely.

Key Facts:
- Requires proper approach speed and configuration.
- Influenced by wind, runway conditions, and aircraft weight.
- Pilots use flaps and landing gear to assist the landing.
- Precision approaches may use systems such as ILS.

Importance:
Landing requires precise aircraft control and is one of the most safety-critical phases of flight.
