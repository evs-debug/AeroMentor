Definition:
The Boeing 787 Dreamliner is a long-range wide-body commercial airliner designed for medium- and long-haul operations.

Specifications:
The aircraft is available in three variants: 787-8, 787-9, and 787-10. It typically cruises at approximately Mach 0.85 and is designed for efficient long-distance travel.

Passenger Capacity:
Depending on the variant and airline configuration, the Boeing 787 can typically accommodate between 242 and 336 passengers.

Range:
The aircraft is capable of long-range operations, with some variants exceeding 14,000 kilometers in range.

Engines:
The Boeing 787 is powered by either General Electric GEnx engines or Rolls-Royce Trent 1000 engines. Both engine families are designed for improved fuel efficiency and reduced emissions.

Fuel Efficiency:
The aircraft achieves significant fuel savings through advanced aerodynamics, lightweight composite materials, and efficient turbofan engines. These technologies reduce fuel burn compared to older wide-body aircraft.

Composite Materials:
Approximately half of the aircraft's primary structure is constructed from composite materials. This reduces weight, improves corrosion resistance, and lowers maintenance requirements.

Passenger Comfort:
The 787 is known for larger windows, improved cabin humidity, lower cabin altitude, and enhanced passenger comfort on long flights.

Importance:
The Boeing 787 introduced a new generation of fuel-efficient long-haul aircraft and has become one of the most successful modern wide-body airliners.
