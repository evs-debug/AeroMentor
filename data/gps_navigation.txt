Definition:
GPS navigation uses satellite signals to determine an aircraft's position and assist navigation.

How It Works:
Aircraft receivers calculate location using signals transmitted from multiple satellites.

Advantages:
- High navigation accuracy.
- Global coverage.
- Reduced dependence on ground-based navigation aids.

Applications:
- En-route navigation.
- Area navigation (RNAV).
- Instrument approaches.

Importance:
GPS has become a primary navigation method in modern aviation.
