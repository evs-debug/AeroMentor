Definition:
The Auxiliary Power Unit (APU) is a small engine that provides power when the main engines are not operating.

Functions:
- Electrical power generation.
- Air conditioning.
- Engine starting assistance.

Key Facts:
- Usually located in the aircraft tail.
- Operates independently of the main engines.
- Common on commercial airliners.

Importance:
The APU allows aircraft systems to operate while on the ground without relying entirely on external power sources.
