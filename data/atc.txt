Definition:
Air Traffic Control (ATC) is a service that manages aircraft movement on the ground and in the air.

How It Works:
Controllers communicate with pilots using radio transmissions to provide instructions, clearances, and traffic information.

Responsibilities:
- Maintain safe aircraft separation.
- Coordinate departures and arrivals.
- Manage airport traffic flow.
- Provide weather and operational information.

Key Facts:
- ATC improves safety and efficiency.
- Controllers monitor aircraft using radar and other surveillance systems.
- Pilots must comply with ATC instructions unless safety is compromised.

Importance:
ATC is essential for preventing collisions and maintaining orderly air traffic operations.
