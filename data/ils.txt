Definition:
The Instrument Landing System (ILS) is a precision approach system used to guide aircraft during landing.

How It Works:
ILS provides lateral guidance through the localizer and vertical guidance through the glideslope.

Components:
- Localizer
- Glideslope
- Marker Beacons or DME

Advantages:
- Enables approaches in low visibility.
- Improves landing accuracy.
- Enhances operational safety.

Importance:
ILS is one of the most widely used precision approach systems in aviation.
