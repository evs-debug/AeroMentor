Definition:
The Boeing 777 is a long-range wide-body twin-engine airliner used on medium- and long-haul routes.

Specifications:
The aircraft is available in several passenger and cargo variants.

Passenger Capacity:
Typical seating ranges from approximately 300 to over 400 passengers.

Range:
Many variants can operate flights exceeding 13,000 kilometers.

Engines:
The aircraft uses high-thrust turbofan engines produced by General Electric, Rolls-Royce, or Pratt & Whitney.

Fuel Efficiency:
Twin-engine efficiency allows the aircraft to operate long routes with lower fuel consumption than older four-engine aircraft.

Importance:
The Boeing 777 is one of the most successful long-haul commercial aircraft ever produced.
