Definition:
The Boeing 777 is a long-range wide-body commercial airliner developed by Boeing.

Key Facts:
- First entered service in 1995.
- One of the largest twin-engine airliners.
- Common variants include the 777-200, 777-300ER and 777X.
- Designed for long-haul international operations.

Importance:
The Boeing 777 demonstrated that large twin-engine aircraft could safely and efficiently perform long-range flights.
