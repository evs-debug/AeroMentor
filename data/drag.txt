Drag is one of the four fundamental forces of flight.

Drag is the aerodynamic force that opposes an aircraft's motion through the air. As an aircraft moves forward, air resistance creates drag and slows the aircraft down.

There are several types of drag, including parasite drag and induced drag. Parasite drag increases with speed, while induced drag is associated with lift generation and is more significant at lower speeds.

To maintain flight, thrust generated by the engines must overcome drag. Aircraft designers continuously work to reduce drag through aerodynamic improvements such as winglets, streamlined fuselages, and smooth surfaces.
