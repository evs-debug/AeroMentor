Definition:
Drag is one of the four fundamental forces of flight.

How It Works:
Drag is the aerodynamic force that opposes an aircraft's motion through the air. Air resistance slows the aircraft and must be overcome by thrust.

Key Facts:
- Parasite drag increases with speed.
- Induced drag is associated with lift generation.
- Streamlined aircraft designs reduce drag.
- Winglets help reduce induced drag.

Importance:
Reducing drag improves fuel efficiency, aircraft performance, and operational range.
