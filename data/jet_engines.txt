Definition:
Jet engines generate thrust by accelerating air rearward.

How It Works:
Air enters the intake, is compressed, mixed with fuel, ignited in the combustion chamber, and expelled through the exhaust to produce thrust.

Major Components:
- Intake
- Compressor
- Combustion Chamber
- Turbine
- Exhaust

Key Facts:
- Most modern airliners use turbofan engines.
- Jet engines are efficient at high speeds.
- Engine efficiency affects fuel consumption.

Importance:
Jet engines provide the thrust necessary for takeoff, climb, cruise, and landing operations.
