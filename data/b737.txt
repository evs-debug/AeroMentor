Definition:
The Boeing 737 is a narrow-body commercial airliner used primarily on short- and medium-haul routes.

Specifications:
The aircraft is available in multiple generations including the Next Generation and MAX families.

Passenger Capacity:
Typical seating ranges from approximately 130 to 230 passengers.

Range:
The aircraft is capable of regional and medium-range operations.

Engines:
Modern variants use efficient CFM International turbofan engines.

Fuel Efficiency:
Improved engines and winglet designs reduce fuel consumption.

Importance:
The Boeing 737 is among the most widely operated commercial aircraft in the world.
