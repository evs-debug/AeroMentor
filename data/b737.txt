Definition:
The Boeing 737 is a narrow-body commercial airliner designed for short- and medium-haul routes.

Key Facts:
- One of the best-selling commercial aircraft in history.
- Commonly used by airlines worldwide.
- Available in several variants including the 737 NG and 737 MAX families.
- Typically seats between 130 and 230 passengers depending on configuration.

Importance:
The Boeing 737 forms the backbone of many airline fleets due to its versatility, efficiency, and widespread support infrastructure.
