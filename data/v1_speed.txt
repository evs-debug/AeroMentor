Definition:
V1 is the takeoff decision speed.

How It Works:
Before reaching V1, a pilot can generally reject the takeoff if a serious issue occurs. After V1, the takeoff should usually continue because insufficient runway may remain for a safe stop.

Factors Affecting V1:
- Aircraft weight
- Runway length
- Weather conditions
- Aircraft performance
- Runway surface conditions

Importance:
V1 is one of the most critical speeds in aviation and is essential for safe takeoff operations.
