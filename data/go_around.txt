Definition:
A go-around is a maneuver where a pilot aborts a landing attempt and climbs away for another approach.

Common Reasons:
- Unstable approach.
- Runway obstruction.
- Poor visibility.
- ATC instruction.

Procedure:
- Apply go-around power.
- Establish a positive climb.
- Follow published procedures.

Importance:
Go-arounds improve safety by preventing unsafe landings.
