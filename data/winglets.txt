Definition:
Winglets are vertical or angled extensions located at the tips of aircraft wings.

How They Work:
Winglets reduce wingtip vortices, which decreases induced drag and improves aerodynamic efficiency.

Benefits:
- Reduced fuel consumption
- Increased range
- Improved climb performance
- Lower emissions

Aircraft Examples:
- Boeing 737
- Boeing 787
- Airbus A350

Importance:
Winglets improve aircraft performance and help airlines reduce operating costs.
