Definition:
Weight is one of the four fundamental forces of flight.

How It Works:
Weight is the force generated by gravity acting on an aircraft. It acts downward toward the center of the Earth.

Key Facts:
- Weight opposes lift.
- Fuel, cargo, and passengers increase weight.
- Aircraft performance changes with weight.
- Heavier aircraft require more lift.

Importance:
Weight affects takeoff distance, climb performance, fuel consumption, and landing performance.
