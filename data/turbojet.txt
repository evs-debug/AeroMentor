Definition:
A turbojet is one of the earliest jet engine designs.

How It Works:
Air passes through the engine core where it is compressed, mixed with fuel, combusted, and expelled through the exhaust.

Key Facts:
- Produces high-speed exhaust.
- Efficient at high speeds.
- Louder and less fuel-efficient than turbofans.

Importance:
Turbojets played a major role in the development of modern jet aviation.
