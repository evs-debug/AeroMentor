Definition:
VOR (VHF Omnidirectional Range) is a ground-based radio navigation system used by aircraft.

How It Works:
A VOR station transmits signals that allow aircraft to determine their bearing relative to the station.

Advantages:
- Reliable navigation aid.
- Widely available.
- Easy to use.

Limitations:
- Requires line-of-sight reception.
- Coverage decreases with distance and terrain obstruction.

Importance:
VOR has been a cornerstone of aviation navigation for decades.
