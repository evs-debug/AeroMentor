Definition:
Thrust is one of the four fundamental forces of flight.

How It Works:
Thrust is generated by aircraft engines and propels the aircraft forward. Jet engines produce thrust by accelerating air rearward.

Key Facts:
- Thrust opposes drag.
- More thrust increases acceleration.
- Engine type affects thrust production.
- Takeoff typically requires high thrust settings.

Importance:
Without sufficient thrust, an aircraft cannot accelerate, climb, or maintain flight.
