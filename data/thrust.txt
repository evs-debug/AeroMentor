Thrust is one of the four fundamental forces of flight.

Thrust is the forward force produced by an aircraft's engines. It allows the aircraft to accelerate and overcome aerodynamic drag.

Jet engines generate thrust by accelerating air rearward. According to Newton's Third Law of Motion, pushing air backward produces an equal and opposite force that pushes the aircraft forward.

The amount of thrust required depends on aircraft weight, altitude, speed, and flight conditions. During takeoff, maximum thrust is often used to achieve sufficient speed for flight.
