Definition:
The Airbus A320 is a narrow-body commercial airliner designed for short- and medium-haul routes.

Specifications:
The aircraft is part of the Airbus A320 family and features advanced fly-by-wire flight controls.

Passenger Capacity:
Most configurations accommodate between 140 and 180 passengers.

Range:
The A320 is designed for regional and medium-range operations.

Engines:
The aircraft may be powered by CFM International or Pratt & Whitney engines depending on variant.

Fuel Efficiency:
Modern engines and aerodynamic improvements contribute to low operating costs.

Importance:
The A320 is one of the most successful and widely used commercial aircraft in aviation history.
