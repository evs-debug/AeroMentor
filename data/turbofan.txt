Definition:
A turbofan engine is the most common engine type used on modern commercial aircraft.

How It Works:
A large fan at the front accelerates a significant volume of air. Some air passes through the engine core while the rest bypasses it.

Advantages:
- High fuel efficiency.
- Lower noise levels.
- Strong thrust production.
- Suitable for commercial aviation.

Importance:
Turbofan engines power most modern passenger airliners worldwide.
