Definition:
The Airbus A380 is a double-deck wide-body airliner designed for high-capacity long-haul routes.

Specifications:
The aircraft is the world's largest passenger airliner.

Passenger Capacity:
Depending on configuration, it can accommodate more than 500 passengers.

Range:
The aircraft is capable of operating ultra-long-haul international routes.

Engines:
The A380 uses Rolls-Royce Trent 900 or Engine Alliance GP7200 engines.

Fuel Efficiency:
Despite its size, advanced aerodynamics and efficient engines improve operating efficiency.

Importance:
The A380 remains one of the most recognizable and technologically advanced passenger aircraft ever built.
