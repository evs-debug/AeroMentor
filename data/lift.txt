Definition:
Lift is one of the four fundamental forces of flight.

How It Works:
Lift is generated when air flows around a wing and creates a pressure difference between the upper and lower surfaces. The lower pressure above the wing and higher pressure below the wing create an upward force.

Key Facts:
- Lift acts perpendicular to the relative airflow.
- Lift increases with airspeed.
- Wing shape influences lift production.
- Angle of attack affects lift generation.

Importance:
Lift must overcome weight for an aircraft to become airborne and remain in flight.
