Definition:
Fly-by-wire is an electronic flight control system that replaces many traditional mechanical control linkages.

How It Works:
Pilot control inputs are converted into electronic signals that command flight control surfaces.

Advantages:
- Reduced weight
- Improved reliability
- Flight envelope protection
- Enhanced aircraft performance

Aircraft Examples:
- Airbus A320
- Airbus A350
- Boeing 787

Importance:
Fly-by-wire technology is a major advancement in modern aircraft design.
