Definition:
Angle of attack is the angle between a wing's chord line and the relative airflow.

How It Works:
As the angle of attack increases, lift generally increases until the critical angle of attack is reached.

Key Facts:
- Every wing has a critical angle of attack.
- Exceeding the critical angle causes a stall.
- Angle of attack is independent of airspeed.
- Pilots monitor angle of attack during critical phases of flight.

Importance:
Understanding angle of attack is essential for preventing stalls and maintaining safe aircraft control.
